✍️ Spiritual Testament Writing Examples
💡 Writing Tip: This is not a legal will, but a letter passing on your faith legacy. Write individually for each child/disciple.
📝 Example 1: To My Child
"Dear Young-Soo,

As I write this, you are 25. The day I first held you feels like yesterday...

There are three things I want to leave you.

First, make Jesus the Lord of your life.
I met Jesus at 30. Before that, I lived by my own strength — proud and lonely. But when I knelt before the cross, I found true freedom. You met Jesus earlier than I did — abide in Him all your life.

Second, practice DCP daily (Scripture, Prayer, Worship, Gratitude, Evangelism).
This changed my life. Hard at first, but now it is my greatest joy. You will face stages 2–3 struggles, but do not give up. Stage 4 joy will surely come.

Third, pass on the grace you have received to the next generation.
I learned faith from Pastor John and passed it to you. Now you must pass this faith to your children and disciples. That is Abraham's covenant and our family's mission.

Finally, honor your mother. She is far more devout than I am.

Until we meet again in heaven, walk in victory in the Lord.

I love you.
— Your Father"
📝 Example 2: To a Spiritual Disciple
"Dear Deacon,

It has been 10 years. I'm grateful I could be by your side during your struggles.

What I ask of you:

1. Pass on EWGF to the next generation. Teach the 5-stage framework we studied together to your disciples. Remember that the stage 2→3 transition is the hardest, and be there so they do not give up.

2. Never stop interceding. When you began praying for 7 people, your life changed. Do not forget that grace — stand as an intercessor for life.

3. I entrust you with our church youth ministry. The ministry I served for 20 years. What they need is not sermons, but a mentor who shows them by example.

I dream of the day we offer crowns to the Lord together in heaven.

In Christ,
Elder Kim"
📝 Example 3: To Church Co-Workers
"Dear beloved members of our church,

The 30 years of laughter, tears, and prayer together have been the greatest gift of my life.

What I want to leave you with:

1. Love one another. Love comes before doctrinal debate. "A new commandment I give you: love one another" — keeping this alone is heaven.

2. Embrace the next generation. Young people leave church because because the church has not answered their questions seriously. Respect their doubts and answer with your lives.

3. Be light in the world. Holiness is not only inside the church. but living out your identity at work, home, and in your neighborhood — that is true worship.

Each one of you is a masterpiece of God. Run to the end as redeemed rulers.

Until we meet again in heaven,
Your fellow worker"
